That success was largely due to its strict border approach: it closed borders in March to almost all non-residents and required international arrivals to quarantine. Except, that is, for air crew. Under Taiwan's previous rules, pilots only needed to quarantine for three days while flight attendants needed to quarantine for five -- reportedly the difference is that the latter group has more person-to-person interaction.
